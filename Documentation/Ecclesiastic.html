Ecclesiastical Activity Ontology

http://vocab.ox.ac.uk/camelot/Ecclesiastic#

The Ecclesiastical Activity Ontology describes entities, activities and the relationships between them for the following ecclesiastical activities: * Baptism * Religoius Confirmation * Religious Confession * Religious Conversion * Membership of an ecclesiastical body * Holding an ecclesiastical office * Disqualification from an ecclesiastical office

Activity

  1. BaptismThe Christian religious rite of sprinkling water on to a person’s forehead or of immersing them in water, symbolizing purification or regeneration and admission to the Christian Church. In many denominations, baptism is performed on young children and is accompanied by name-giving.

      1. DisqualifiedFromEcclesiasticalOfficeActivity where a person is disqualified ie declared ineligible for an ecclesiastical office.

          1. HoldEcclesiasticalOfficeThe activity of a person holding an ecclesiastical office, i.e. a position of authority or service, typically one of a public nature.

              1. MembershipEcclesiasticalBodyThe activity of a person being a member of an ecclesiastical body.

                  1. OrdinationThe action of making (someone) a priest or minister; confer holy orders.

                      1. PreachDeliver a sermon or religious address to an assembled group of people, typically in church.

                          1. ReligiousConfirmationConfirmation is a rite of initiation in several Christian denominations, normally carried out through anointing, the laying on of hands, and prayer, for the purpose of bestowing the Gift of the Holy Spirit.

                              1. ReligiousConversionThe adoption, wholesale, of a set of beliefs identified with one particular religious denomination to the exclusion of others. Religious conversion marks a transformation of religious identity and is symbolized by special rituals. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_conversion

                                  1. ReligousConfessionConfession is the acknowledgment of sin (or one's sinfulness) or wrongs. It is a religious practice in a number of faith traditions.

                                      1. Membership(defined at: http://www.w3.org/ns/org#Membership)
